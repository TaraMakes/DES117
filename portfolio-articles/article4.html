Collaborative Web App

Balanced Weather

Balanced Weather is a web app where I got to play the role of visual designer and work with a student from the Computer Science Department who would apply my design to a real web app connected to Yahooâ€™s weather API.

My design decisions were informed by my own habits. I check the weather late at night before going to bed, and found that the color palettes on most weather sites are extremely harsh on the eyes at 2 am in the morning. I decided to go with an all orange scheme, because orange does not interrupt the production of norepinephrine, which is a hormone that affects wake-and-sleep schedules. This would lead to less interrupted sleep and an improved quality of life.

Overall, the visual design was an exercise in play, and clear and legible typography. I used Sketch for experimenting with typography. I also worked within the constraints of web design while using icons from the Noun Project.

The hardest part about the project was communicating my design intentions to beginning developers. Many of the students were unfamiliar with design terms and I had to explain in great detail my intentions behind the site. However, I learned that collaboration can be incredibly fun.
